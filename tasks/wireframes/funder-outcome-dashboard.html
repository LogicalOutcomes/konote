Theory of Change

Youth aged 16-24 who are unemployed or underemployed participate in a 12-week job readiness program. Through skills training (weeks 1-6), mentorship matching (weeks 4-8), and supported job search (weeks 8-12), participants develop employment competencies.

Training → Skills acquisition → Confidence → Job interviews → Employment → Income stability

Counterfactual: What Would Have Happened Without This Program?

Baseline: Employment Ontario Services Only

Without the program, participants would access basic Employment Ontario services (job boards, resume review) but not structured training or mentorship. Comparable GTA youth programs show 23% placement without structured support.

Impact Risks & Mitigations

Economic downturn reduces available positions

Likelihood: Medium Severity: High

A recession could reduce entry-level positions, making placement harder despite participant readiness.

Mitigation: Diversify employer partnerships across sectors (12 partners in retail, hospitality, tech, healthcare, nonprofit). Maintain relationships with social enterprises.

Participant attrition due to housing instability

Likelihood: Medium Severity: Medium

Youth experiencing housing instability may not maintain consistent attendance in the 12-week program.

Mitigation: Partner with 3 local shelters for transit passes and flexible scheduling. Offer hybrid attendance for participants in precarious housing.
